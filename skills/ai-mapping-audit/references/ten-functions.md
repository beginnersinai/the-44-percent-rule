# The 10 Production Functions — AI Mapping Framework

Based on Kim, Kim & Koning (2026), these are the 10 core production functions where AI can create value in any business. Most firms only deploy AI in 1-2 of these (usually Content Production and Marketing). The mapping problem is discovering value in the other 8.

---

## 1. Product Development

**What it covers**: Building, improving, testing, and iterating on your core product or service.

**AI applications**:
- Synthetic user testing — AI simulates different customer personas interacting with your product
- Automated quality assurance — AI checks for bugs, inconsistencies, or issues
- Feature impact prediction — AI estimates which features will drive the most value
- Prototype generation — AI creates quick mockups or MVPs for testing
- User feedback analysis — AI categorizes and prioritizes customer feedback
- Competitive feature tracking — AI monitors competitor products for changes

**Signs AI is absent**: You manually test everything, prioritize features by gut feel, don't monitor competitors systematically.

---

## 2. Strategy & Planning

**What it covers**: Business decisions, competitive positioning, roadmaps, market analysis.

**AI applications**:
- Competitive intelligence — AI continuously monitors competitor activity
- Market trend analysis — AI identifies emerging patterns in your industry
- Scenario modeling — AI generates best/worst/likely case projections
- Strategic option evaluation — AI analyzes trade-offs between different paths
- OKR tracking and adjustment — AI monitors progress and suggests pivots
- Decision documentation — AI captures context and rationale for decisions

**Signs AI is absent**: You make strategic decisions based on occasional manual research, don't track competitors systematically, don't model scenarios.

---

## 3. Marketing & Growth

**What it covers**: Customer acquisition, brand building, audience growth, social media.

**AI applications**:
- SEO optimization — AI analyzes keywords, suggests content gaps, monitors rankings
- Ad copy generation and testing — AI creates variants and predicts performance
- Audience targeting — AI identifies highest-value customer segments
- Growth experiment design — AI suggests and designs A/B tests
- Conversion funnel analysis — AI identifies where prospects drop off
- Referral program optimization — AI identifies best incentive structures

**Signs AI is absent**: You manually write all ad copy, don't A/B test, don't analyze your funnel systematically.

---

## 4. Sales & Revenue

**What it covers**: Converting prospects to customers, deal management, pricing.

**AI applications**:
- Lead scoring — AI ranks prospects by conversion likelihood
- Outreach personalization — AI tailors messages to each prospect's context
- Pricing optimization — AI tests different price points and packages
- Revenue forecasting — AI predicts future revenue based on pipeline
- Deal prioritization — AI identifies which opportunities to focus on
- Proposal generation — AI creates customized proposals

**Signs AI is absent**: You price by intuition, don't score leads, write the same generic outreach to everyone.

---

## 5. Customer Success & Retention

**What it covers**: Keeping customers, increasing satisfaction, reducing churn.

**AI applications**:
- Churn prediction — AI identifies customers likely to leave
- Engagement scoring — AI measures how actively customers use your product
- Personalized re-engagement — AI creates targeted win-back campaigns
- Satisfaction analysis — AI analyzes feedback for sentiment and themes
- Support automation — AI handles common questions and routes complex ones
- Customer journey optimization — AI identifies friction points in the experience

**Signs AI is absent**: You treat all customers the same, don't predict churn, don't segment by engagement.

---

## 6. Finance & Pricing

**What it covers**: Revenue tracking, cost management, financial planning, pricing strategy.

**AI applications**:
- Revenue attribution — AI traces which activities drive the most revenue
- Cost optimization — AI identifies unnecessary expenses and cheaper alternatives
- Cash flow forecasting — AI predicts future cash positions
- Pricing intelligence — AI monitors competitor pricing and suggests adjustments
- Financial reporting automation — AI generates regular financial summaries
- ROI analysis — AI calculates return on different investments and activities

**Signs AI is absent**: You don't know which content/product drives the most revenue, don't forecast cash flow, price by copying competitors.

---

## 7. Operations & Logistics

**What it covers**: Day-to-day business execution, workflows, process management.

**AI applications**:
- Workflow automation — AI handles repetitive operational tasks
- Resource scheduling — AI optimizes how time and resources are allocated
- Process optimization — AI identifies bottlenecks and suggests improvements
- SOP generation — AI documents best practices and procedures
- Bottleneck detection — AI identifies what's slowing down production
- Vendor/tool evaluation — AI compares options and recommends solutions

**Signs AI is absent**: You manually manage all workflows, don't track where time goes, have no documented SOPs.

---

## 8. Content Production

**What it covers**: Creating content assets — writing, video, audio, design, etc.

**AI applications**:
- Article/newsletter writing — AI drafts, edits, or enhances content
- Video scripting — AI creates video scripts and storyboards
- Image generation — AI creates visual assets
- Content repurposing — AI transforms content across formats (article → video → social)
- Editing and quality control — AI reviews content for errors, tone, clarity
- Content calendar management — AI plans and schedules content production

**Note**: This is where most firms ALREADY use AI. If this is your only AI function, you have the mapping problem.

---

## 9. Data & Analytics

**What it covers**: Measuring performance, analyzing data, generating insights.

**AI applications**:
- Dashboard generation — AI creates visual reports from raw data
- Anomaly detection — AI flags unusual patterns (traffic spikes, revenue drops)
- Trend identification — AI spots emerging patterns before they're obvious
- Cohort analysis — AI groups users/customers for comparative analysis
- Predictive analytics — AI forecasts future performance based on historical data
- Natural language querying — AI lets you ask questions about your data in plain English

**Signs AI is absent**: You look at raw numbers, don't get alerts for anomalies, can't easily answer "what's driving X?"

---

## 10. System Administration

**What it covers**: Maintaining tools, infrastructure, and the AI systems themselves.

**AI applications**:
- System health monitoring — AI tracks uptime, error rates, performance
- Failure analysis — AI diagnoses what went wrong when things break
- Cost tracking — AI monitors how much your AI tools and infrastructure cost
- Self-optimization — AI analyzes its own output quality and improves
- Configuration management — AI maintains and updates system configurations
- Security monitoring — AI watches for suspicious activity or vulnerabilities

**Signs AI is absent**: You don't know your AI system's failure rate, don't track costs, never analyze whether your AI is actually working well.

---

## Quick Assessment Checklist

Count how many functions have active AI usage:
- **8-10**: Exceptional — you're ahead of 99% of firms
- **5-7**: Good — focus on the remaining gaps
- **3-4**: Average — you have a moderate mapping problem
- **1-2**: Classic mapping problem — the paper's research applies directly to you
- **0**: You haven't started — but the upside is enormous
